Marlin Firmware

Marlin is an Open Source firmware for 3D printers based on AVR microprocessors such as those used on the Arduino platform, it allows full control over the 3D printer, extrusors and heated building plate either by being controlled from a host PC or in a standalone mode.

Learn more »

Rich G-Code Support

G-Code is the instruction code that tells a 3D printer what to do. Marlin's G-Code is loosely based on the NIST standard introducing the required changes for operating a 3D printer.

View details »

A sea of boards

Marlin supports a wide variety of 3D printers, including all RAMPS variants, and is adaptable to virtually any Arduino-based electronics through pin-mapping.

View details »

Automatic Leveling

You'll never have to fine tune the inclination of the table, Marlin will do all the hard work for you, just select one from the multiple available probing options and execute a G29 before printing.

View details »

 

Thermal Protection

Thermal Runaway Protection protects your printer from damage and fire if a thermistor falls out or temperature sensors fail in any way.

View details »

Multiple Kinematics

Marlin supports Delta, SCARA, and Cartesian kinematics.

USB Printing

You can connect Marlin to Octoprint, Slic3r, Cura, Simplify 3D or any other host software which supports USB serial printing.
