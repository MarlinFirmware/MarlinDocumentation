Input Shaping Calibration Pattern

Based on the excellent work of @tombrazier

{% alert warning %} Caution! Only for Cartesian printers. CoreXY kinematics are not yet tested. {% endalert %}

Use this form to generate G-code that you can use to calibrate your Input Shaping Damping Frequency and Zeta/Damping factor. Default values apply to standard PLA with a 0.4mm nozzle.

The generated G-code files print frequency sweeps (zigzag patterns with increasing speeds) to find resonant frequencies (frequencies to dampen) and zeta/damping factors for X-axis and Y-axis.

3 separate files are generated for download:

    Damping Frequency to find resonant frequency for X-axis and Y-axis (shown on right side)
    Zeta/damping factor for X-axis (not shown)
    Zeta/damping factor for Y-axis (not shown)

Press Generate G-code followed by each Download * as file to save the results.

Notes:

  • Max X Frequency and Max Y Frequency should be high enough to reach resonant frequency. The moving bed of a "bedslinger" printer usually has a lower resonant frequency corresponding to its greater mass. Adjust values after your first test print and as-needed to match the printer.
  • Use Bed Leveling requires a probe.
  • Printer and other parts (e.g., filament spool) may resonate at several frequencies.
  • Printer may skip layers at or above the resonant frequency. This is normal behaviour.
  • 60Hz frequency equals 120mm/s print speed. With default 0.5mm line width and 0.2mm layer height, that equals to 12mm^3/s extrusion flow. Nozzle Temperature can be raised to increase extrusion flow.

Damping Frequency Pattern example 1

  • Measure the distance (mm) on the X axis to the position where amplitude increases or the pattern loses its shape. The damping frequency for Y is this distance divided by 2.
  • Measure the distance (mm) on the Y axis along the right side to the position where the amplitude increases or the pattern loses its shape. The damping frequency for X is this distance divided by 2.
